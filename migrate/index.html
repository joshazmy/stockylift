Migrating off Stocky? Start here.

Shopify's Stocky shuts down — app and API — on August 31, 2026. This hub covers how to export stocky suppliers and purchase orders before the stocky api sunset, how StockyLift compares to the full inventory-planning apps merchants move to, and step-by-step guides for importing your data into each one.

StockyLift vs. the inventory-planning apps

StockyLift is not trying to be Prediko, Inventory Planner, Genie, or Sumtracker. It is the tool that gets your Stocky supplier and PO data out, in a form those apps can import. Here is how they actually compare:

ToolPricing modelWhat it isLearn more
StockyLift One-time $39 (export tool, not a subscription) A Chrome extension that exports your Stocky supplier & purchase-order data to CSV/JSON before the Aug 31, 2026 API sunset — the escape hatch out of Stocky, not a replacement inventory system. Get StockyLift →
Prediko Subscription (recurring monthly/annual plans) A full inventory-planning and demand-forecasting platform for Shopify merchants — purchase orders, supplier records, forecasting, and reordering, billed as an ongoing subscription. Find Prediko on the Shopify App Store →
Inventory Planner Subscription (recurring monthly/annual plans) An inventory forecasting and replenishment platform with supplier and purchase-order management, billed as an ongoing subscription. Find Inventory Planner on the Shopify App Store →
Genie Subscription (recurring monthly plans) An inventory and purchase-order management app for Shopify, sold as an ongoing subscription rather than a one-time export tool. Find Genie on the Shopify App Store →
Sumtracker Subscription (recurring monthly/annual plans) An inventory, bundling, and purchase-order management platform for Shopify and multi-channel sellers, billed as an ongoing subscription. Find Sumtracker on the Shopify App Store →
